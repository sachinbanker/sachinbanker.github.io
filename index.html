About

My research centers on understanding consumer judgment and decision making by applying methods from behavioral economics, neuroeconomics, and computational social science. I am particularly interested in topics related to consumer behavior in the digital marketplace, where I study how people interact with online recommendation systems, new payment technologies, and social media.

I am currently Assistant Professor of Marketing at the Eccles School of Business, University of Utah. Previously I completed my doctorate at the MIT Sloan School of Management and postdoctoral work at the Princeton University School of Public and International Affairs.
